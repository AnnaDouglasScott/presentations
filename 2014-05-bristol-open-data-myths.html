Myth 0

open data == personal data

image credit: ulrich atz

Myth 1

accessible data == open data

 

would you resell content from e.g. bbc.co.uk?

that "open api" is probably not open data

open data must have an open license

http://opendefinition.org/licenses/

Myth 2

open data == open gov data

Myth 3

open data is just
about transparency etc

 

...and compelling business reasons!

some concrete numbers on ROI

Myth 4

opening data means
giving away the crown jewels

 

depends on the nature of your business

Myth 5

you have to open everything

 

just open a small slice of data in the first instance

(could be a horizontal or vertical slice)

Myth 6

if you release it, they will come

 

need to concentrate on releasing data that is useful to people...

...and making it easy for them to use it

Myth 7

you gotta have a hackathon

 

even rocky had a hackathon

Myth 8

Creative Commons licenses
are the only fruit

 

http://opendefinition.org/licenses/

Myth 9

anonymisation is easy

 

not always true

but there are people that can help (UKAN)

Myth 10

open data is just too hard

 

one step at a time

open data certificates will hold your hand

opendatamonitor project: will make it easier to understand what's out there

dapaas project: will make it easier to publish more
